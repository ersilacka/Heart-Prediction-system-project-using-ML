# Heart-Prediction-system-project-using-ML
# Introduction
Heart attacks are a major global health concern, and early risk identification can significantly reduce their impact through preventive care. With the help of machine learning and medical data, it is possible to estimate an individual’s risk of experiencing a heart attack. This project aims to develop a heart attack risk prediction system that provides a percentage‑based risk estimate instead of a simple yes/no result.

# Problem Statement
The objective of this project is to build a system that predicts the probability (%) of a heart attack occurring within the next 10 years based on demographic, clinical, and lifestyle information. A percentage‑based output offers a more informative and realistic assessment of risk than a binary decision.

# Prediction Objective
The system predicts a numerical risk value between 0 and 100, representing the likelihood of a heart attack within a 10‑year period. The result is also categorized into risk levels such as Low, Moderate, or High to improve interpretability.
Target Population
The model is intended for adults aged 30 to 75 years with no prior history of heart attack. It is designed for general preventive risk assessment rather than emergency or diagnostic use.

# Input Features
The prediction system uses commonly available health indicators, including:

Age and sex
Blood pressure
Cholesterol levels
Smoking status
Diabetes status
Use of blood pressure medication
These features are well‑established risk factors for heart disease.

# Output Definition
The output consists of a heart attack risk percentage along with a corresponding risk category. This format allows users to better understand their level of risk and supports preventive decision‑making.

# Conclusion
This step defines the problem and scope of the heart attack risk prediction system. By focusing on a 10‑year risk percentage, the project establishes a strong foundation for data selection, preprocessing, and model development in subsequent steps.
